The Problem

Safety Criticality

Autonomous vehicles must interpret regulatory signals (Speed limits, Stop, Yield) with 98% reliability. A missed sign is a critical safety failure.

The "Tunnel Vision" Issue

Our current prototype uses a Fixed Central Crop. While accurate, it cannot detect signs located on the periphery (sidewalks). This is the primary issue addressed in our roadmap.

Data & Preprocessing

1. Data Source

Collected from Kaggle: The German Traffic Sign Recognition Benchmark (GTSRB).

  • 43 Classes of distinct signs.
  • 50,000+ Labeled Images.

2. Preprocessing

Preparing raw data for the CNN architecture.

  • Resizing: All images scaled to 30x30 pixels.
  • Color: Standardized to 3-channel RGB.

3. Augmentation

Enhancing dataset robustness using ImageDataGenerator.

  • Operations: Rotation, Zoom, Shift.
  • Goal: Generalize against real-world variance.

Computer Vision Integration

1. Capture & Crop

Using OpenCV to grab frames. System automatically crops to a center 300px ROI to mimic "focus".

Tech: cv2.VideoCapture

2. Color Correction

Issue: OpenCV uses BGR, Model trained on RGB. Red signs looked blue!

Fix: cv2.cvtColor(BGR2RGB)

3. Jitter Control

Predictions flickered due to sensor noise. We added a buffer to smooth outputs.

Fix: Deque Buffer (10 Frames)

Technical Roadmap

1. Object Detection (Localization)

Goal: Move from fixed-crop to dynamic detection.
Tech: Integrate YOLOv8 or SSD to scan the full 1080p frame and find signs anywhere.

2. High-Resolution Model

Goal: Recognize signs at 50m+ distance.
Tech: Retrain classifier on 64x64 or 128x128 inputs to capture details of small signs.

3. Edge Optimization

Goal: Real-time embedded performance.
Tech: Convert to TensorFlow Lite (TFLite) with 8-bit Quantization for < 30ms latency.
